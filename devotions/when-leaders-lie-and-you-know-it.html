When Leaders Lie and You Know It

Ezekiel 34:2

Scripture:

“Son of man, prophesy against the shepherds of Israel; prophesy and say to them, thus says the Lord God: Woe to the shepherds of Israel who feed themselves. Should not the shepherds feed the sheep?” Ezekiel 34:2

Devotion:

Moments exist when leadership speaks loudly yet truth feels absent. Words may sound right, yet something within recognizes misalignment.

Clarity in those moments does not equal rebellion. Recognition of wrong reflects discernment shaped by truth.

Jesus never protected false leadership. He exposed it. Appearance never outweighed authenticity. Titles never replaced truth.

When leadership chooses image over integrity, confusion follows. Trust weakens. Truth becomes uncomfortable. Still, God remains unchanged.

Peace grows through staying anchored in His Word. Strength develops through refusing to defend what is known to be wrong. Response remains rooted in love, not retaliation.

God continues to lead, even when people fail.

Prayer:

Lord Jesus, healing is needed for places where truth has been misrepresented. Restore clarity within my heart and guard me from bitterness.

Help me walk in truth with humility, strength, and wisdom. Keep my focus on You as the Good Shepherd who never fails.

Lead me forward with peace, even when others fall short. In Jesus’ name, amen.

Final Truth:

When leaders fail, Jesus still leads. His presence remains anchored in truth, not position.

Story Time:

The Day I Stopped Clapping

I remember Sunday like it was yesterday. The sanctuary was full, the choir was loud, and the pastor was preaching what sounded like truth, yet something within me would not settle.

That morning carried a heaviness. Not from sin, though from silence. Things had been seen. Things had been heard. They did not align with the Jesus found in Scripture.

No one spoke. I stayed quiet too. Until that day.

The message centered on integrity. I wanted to receive it. Still, reality could not be ignored.

The room responded with applause.

I remained still.

No agreement. No response. Only silence. In that moment, something shifted. Not anger. Clarity.

God was not asking for agreement. God was asking for awareness.

“Woe to the shepherds who only take care of themselves.” Ezekiel 34:2

That day marked a change. Defense of what was wrong ended. Scripture became the anchor. Prayer became the response.

Freedom followed, slowly and steadily.

Jesus remained the Good Shepherd.

If this feels familiar, you are not alone. Clarity is not rebellion. It is awakening.

Moral:

Truth does not lose authority when people misuse it. Keep following Jesus.
