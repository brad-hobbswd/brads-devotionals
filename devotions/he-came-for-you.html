He Came for You

John 4:13–14

Scripture:

“Jesus answered her, all who drink of this water will be thirsty again. But whoever drinks the water that I will give him shall never, not ever, be thirsty anymore. But the water that I will give him shall become a spring of water welling up (flowing, bubbling) continually within him unto eternal life.” John 4:13–14

Devotion:

Jesus did not come to the well for water. He came for her. The same truth remains for you.

The Samaritan woman carried more than a water jar. Her life held rejection, weariness, and a past that shaped how others saw her. Her present felt isolated, and her future likely appeared unchanged, until Jesus entered that moment.

He met her exactly where she was, not within a place of worship, though within an ordinary and lonely setting. A simple request opened the conversation, yet His intention reached far deeper than water. His desire was restoration.

Jesus offered living water, something beyond physical need, something that reaches into the soul. Nothing about her past disqualified her from receiving what He gave. The moment was never about her history. The moment was about her heart.

The same invitation remains. Jesus steps into dry places, hidden places, and quiet places. His presence does not come to shame or expose. His presence comes to restore, to fill, and to give what nothing else can provide.

When you encounter Him, what once felt necessary begins to lose its hold. Old patterns release. Empty wells are left behind. A new life begins to form through His presence.

Prayer:

Lord Jesus, gratitude rises for the way You meet me where I am, not with judgment, though with grace. You see every part of my life and still choose to come near.

Fill every empty place within me with Your living water. Replace what has never satisfied with Your presence that restores and sustains. Teach my heart to rest in You and to receive what only You can give.

Let my life reflect the change that comes from truly encountering You. In Jesus’ name, amen.
