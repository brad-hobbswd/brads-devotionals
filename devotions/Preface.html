Preface

The story behind the journey

I never planned to write a devotional like this.

Growing up as the child of a prophet, I was raised under the weight of spiritual expectation. I knew the language of church. I understood the structure, the flow of service, and the doctrines, including the precepts of what many called the Trinity. Something deep inside me never quite settled.

Questions remained within me, not out of rebellion, though out of a deep desire to understand. Responses often arrived through silence, deflection, or shame. Curiosity began to feel like disqualification, as though faith required quiet compliance while something within continued to whisper that more truth existed.

That search led me to Jesus.

Jesus revealed not as one part of a divided Godhead, though as God Himself, the fullness of the Godhead bodily (Colossians 2:9). Not a mystery to avoid, though a revelation to embrace. Not many, though One Lord, One faith, One baptism (Ephesians 4:5).

This devotional was born out of that journey.

Raised within the presence of power, brokenness still found its way through people who claimed to speak for God. Service came easily. Expression came naturally. Presence was consistent. Yet something within still felt unseen and left behind. Personal encounter with Jesus and His truth marked the beginning of healing.

This book is for the wounded, the silenced, and the overlooked. For those taught what to believe without being invited to understand. For those recovering from church hurt, spiritual pressure, or unanswered questions. For those who have felt distant within spaces that should have embraced them.

Each devotion is rooted in scripture and shaped through honesty. Prayer rests in hope that these words will walk gently with you through healing, awakening, and truth revealed through the One who never confused, never rejected, and never required distance to be reached. His name is Jesus. He is not part of the story. He is the story.

“Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God is one Lord.” Deuteronomy 6:4
“And ye are complete in Him, which is the head of all principality and power.” Colossians 2:10

Welcome to a journey shaped by healing, truth, and the revelation of Jesus.

With love,
Bradley
