When the Foolish Become Favored

1 Corinthians 1:27‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬‬

Devotion:

God doesn’t operate by human logic. While the world celebrates power, intellect, and prestige, God delights in using what seems insignificant to display His glory. He uses the overlooked, the underestimated, the broken, and the unqualified, so that no one can boast, but all must point back to Him.

Think about it:

• He used a shepherd boy named David to slay a giant and become king.
• He chose uneducated fishermen to spread the Gospel to the nations.
• He allowed a donkey to speak to correct a prophet.
• He saved the world through a crucified Savior, a method the world would call foolish.

Why? Because in our weakness, His strength is perfected. When the world says you’re not enough, God says, “You’re exactly who I want to use.”

Prophetic Word
God is raising up the least likely.

Those who’ve been laughed at, left out, and labeled, get ready. What was once called foolish will be seen as favored. He is preparing to confound the “wise” systems, religious norms, and worldly strategies through voices no one expected. Yours might be one of them.

Prayer:

Lord, thank You for choosing the foolish things, sometimes, that’s me. I don’t always feel strong, wise, or qualified, but I trust that You know what You’re doing. Use me for Your glory. Let Your wisdom shine through my weakness. In Jesus’ name, amen.
