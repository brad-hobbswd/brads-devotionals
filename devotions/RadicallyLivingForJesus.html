Radically Living for Jesus

Acts 1:8

Scripture:

“But you shall receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you, and you shall be My witnesses…” Acts 1:8

Devotion:

Living for Jesus calls for full surrender. Faith was never meant to remain partial or hidden. A life yielded to Him becomes transformed from the inside out.

The Spirit fills, empowers, and directs. That power is not for appearance. That power produces change. Desires shift. Priorities realign. Purpose becomes clear.

Jesus works like a refining fire. He removes what does not belong and strengthens what reflects Him. That process shapes a life that carries His presence into every environment.

Faith becomes visible through action. Words align with truth. Choices reflect conviction. Daily life turns into a testimony.

People may never open a Bible, yet they will read your life. What they see matters.

Boldness grows through the Spirit. Fear loses influence when identity is rooted in Christ. Courage rises when obedience becomes the focus.

Every place becomes an assignment. Home. School. Work. Community. Each one holds opportunity to reflect Jesus clearly.

Challenge:

• Is every area of your life surrendered to Jesus?
• Does your life reflect His truth daily?
• Where is God calling you to live more boldly?

Prayer:

Lord Jesus, refine my life and shape my heart. Fill me with Your Spirit and strengthen me to live boldly for You. Let every part of my life reflect Your truth and Your love. Use me as a witness in every place You have positioned me. In Jesus’ name, amen.

Story Time

The High School Student Who Refused to Blend In

Marcus was a senior in high school. He loved basketball, spent time with friends, and lived a normal student life. Everything shifted when he fully surrendered his life to Jesus.

He could no longer blend in. Conversations changed. Choices changed. His response to everyday situations changed.

In the locker room, he walked away from crude talk. On weekends, he stepped away from parties and began gathering students for Bible study during lunch.

Mockery followed. Some friendships faded. Comments were made. Pressure increased.

Marcus stayed steady.

He continued showing up. He continued praying. He continued living his faith openly.

One day, a teammate who once mocked him came in broken. His family was falling apart, and he had nowhere to turn.

In the middle of the gym, Marcus prayed with him. The same students who once laughed now stood in silence, watching something real unfold.

That moment shifted everything.

By the end of the year, students gathered regularly. What started with one decision turned into a movement that touched many lives.

Moral:

Bold faith may cost comfort, approval, or relationships, yet it produces eternal impact. When a life is fully surrendered, even ordinary environments become places where God moves powerfully.
